# Subject Line Formulas & Best Practices

## Proven Formula Patterns

### 1. Curiosity Gap
"[Surprising fact] — here's what it means for [audience]"
- "73% of emails get opened for this one reason"
- "The inbox trick marketers don't talk about"

### 2. How-To
"How to [achieve desired outcome] in [timeframe]"
- "How to boost open rates in 30 days"
- "How to write emails that actually convert"

### 3. List/Number
"[Number] [things] to [achieve outcome]"
- "5 subject line mistakes killing your open rate"
- "3 ways to improve your email design today"

### 4. Question
"[Question that reader wants answered]?"
- "Is your email landing in spam?"
- "Ready to see your best campaign yet?"

### 5. Personalization
"[Name], [relevant statement]"
- "[Name], your monthly insights are ready"
- "[Name], we picked these for you"

### 6. Urgency (Non-Spammy)
"[Time-bound element] + [value]"
- "Your early access ends Friday"
- "New arrivals — first look for members"

### 7. Social Proof
"[Number/Authority] [trust signal]"
- "Join 10,000+ marketers who read this"
- "What award-winning campaigns have in common"

### 8. Benefit-First
"[Clear benefit]: [supporting detail]"
- "Higher conversions: the template that works"
- "Save 3 hours a week on email production"

## Length Guidelines

| Platform | Optimal Length | Max Before Truncation |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|
| Desktop (Gmail, Outlook) | 40-60 chars | ~70 chars |
| Mobile (iOS Mail) | 30-40 chars | ~40 chars |
| Mobile (Gmail app) | 35-45 chars | ~45 chars |
| Apple Watch | 15-20 chars | ~20 chars |

**Rule of thumb:** Put the most important words in the first 30 characters.

## A/B Testing Insights

- Questions outperform statements by 10-15% on average
- Numbers outperform no-number subjects by 8-12%
- Personalization ([Name]) lifts open rates 5-10%
- Emoji in position 1 or last can lift 3-5% (industry-dependent)
- Lowercase subjects outperform Title Case by 3-5%
- Short (< 40 chars) outperform long (> 70 chars) by 12%

## What to Avoid

- ALL CAPS words (triggers spam filters)
- Clickbait without payoff (damages trust)
- Re: or Fwd: prefix (deceptive)
- Multiple punctuation (!!!, ???)
- Generic phrases ("Newsletter #42", "Monthly Update")
- Misleading content that doesn't match the email body

## Preheader Pairing

The preheader should complement, not repeat, the subject line:

| Subject | Bad Preheader | Good Preheader |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| "Your June recap is here" | "Your June recap" | "3 wins, 1 insight, and a surprise" |
| "New features just launched" | "We launched new features" | "The one you've been asking for is finally here" |
| "5 tips for better emails" | "Read our 5 tips" | "Tip #3 changed everything for us" |
